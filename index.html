Getting here

Easy to reach, hard to leave.

The resort sits right off Vaibhavwadi Road in Talere — close to local transport, and within a comfortable drive of the region's stations.

Talere Bus Stand

~2 km · local

The nearest ST bus stop, right in the village — the easiest hop for local and short-distance travel.

Kankavali Railway Station

~10 km approx · Konkan Railway

A major stop on the Konkan Railway line, with regular express and mail trains — the main station for the resort.

Vaibhavwadi Road Station

~35 km approx · Konkan Railway

A secondary Konkan Railway stop further along NH-66 — useful for a wider range of train timings.

Day trips from Talere

The coast keeps going.

Sindhudurg's forts, temples, and beaches are all an easy there and back from the resort including a few spots that have only recently started drawing crowds.

~45 km · ~1h 15m

Malvan & Tarkarli

Sindhudurg Fort rises straight out of the sea, and Tarkarli's backwaters run clear enough for snorkelling and glass-bottom boat rides.

Best for: a full beach day
~44 km · ~1h 10m

Vijaydurg Fort

An 800-year-old sea-fort with a submerged wall built to wreck approaching ships — far quieter than Malvan.

Best for: history & sea views
~48 km · ~1h 20m

Devgad

The home ground of the original Alphonso mango, with a working harbour, a lighthouse, and beaches that stay empty even in season.

Best for: mango orchards, slow beaches
~55 km · ~1h 30m

Tondavali "Tsunami Island"

A sandbar that appears at low tide, walkable out into the sea — one of the Konkan's newest and most-photographed spots.

Trending now
~95–100 km · ~2.5h

Amboli Ghat & Waterfalls

A misty hill-station detour inland — waterfalls, dense forest, and a cooler climate, especially striking in monsoon.

Best for: waterfalls & monsoon views
~100–120 km · ~2.5h

Ratnagiri

Further north, but worth the longer drive — Ganpatipule's beach temple, the old Ratnagiri lighthouse, and Thibaw Palace.

Best for: a full-day coastal drive
